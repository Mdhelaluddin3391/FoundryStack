What We Build

MVP Development

This is our flagship service for founders who need to launch a product that is both fast to market and built to last.

Solves the problem of: Needing to validate a business idea with real users without accumulating technical debt that requires a future rewrite.

Example Use-Cases:
  • SaaS applications (B2B or B2C)
  • E-commerce platforms with custom logic
  • Two-sided marketplaces and booking systems
  • Internal tools and company dashboards

Architecture & Code Review

For founders with an existing product who are facing bugs, slow performance, or difficulty adding new features.

Solves the problem of: An existing codebase that has become a "black box" or a "big ball of mud," hindering growth and new feature development.

Example Use-Cases:
  • Auditing a struggling v1.0 before scaling
  • Planning a migration from a monolith to services
  • Due diligence before acquiring a technical asset
  • Improving code quality for team hand-off

Fractional CTO Retainer

Ongoing technical leadership for founders who need strategic guidance without the cost of a full-time executive hire.

Solves the problem of: Making critical technology decisions (hiring, architecture, scaling) without an experienced, unbiased technical leader in your corner.

Example Use-Cases:
  • Guiding long-term technical roadmaps
  • Interviewing and vetting engineering candidates
  • Overseeing complex technical projects
  • Advising on security, compliance, and infrastructure

What's Included in Every Project

We believe in full transparency and complete ownership.

  • Complete source code and IP ownership.
  • A clean, modular architecture diagram.
  • Database schema and data model documentation.
  • Deployment scripts for your hosting environment.
  • A private staging server for you to track progress.
  • 30 days of post-launch support for bug fixes.

What's Not Included by Default

To keep scopes clear and focused, these items are separate.

  • User interface (UI) or visual design.
  • Landing page or marketing content creation.
  • Ongoing hosting, domain, or third-party API costs.
  • User acquisition or digital marketing services.
  • Major feature additions after the initial scope is complete.
